# Data Notes and Interpretation Rules

## Why evidence labels are used instead of native/not native

Fungi are not documented like vascular plants. For plants, a county-level native status may be available through plant range systems. For fungi, that kind of clean county nativity source usually does not exist. Many fungi live underground, inside wood, on roots, on leaves, or as microscopic associates. Some are visible only briefly after rain. Some records are old, misidentified, or affected by changing taxonomy.

Because of that, this dashboard uses evidence-strength labels rather than a simple native/not-native field.

## Occurrence evidence ladder

- **5 — Strong local evidence:** preserved specimen, voucher, or equivalent local record.
- **4 — Strong mapped evidence:** coordinate-confirmed or refined Hays County evidence.
- **3 — Good local candidate:** iNaturalist checklist, Hays-area observation, or candidate evidence.
- **2 — Regional candidate:** Texas/Central Texas/bounding-box evidence that needs exact review.
- **1 — Relationship known only:** host, interaction, or function evidence but not local occurrence proof.
- **0 — Needs more study:** insufficient support in the merged source layers.

## Source layer roles

- **MyCoPortal:** strongest specimen/collection evidence backbone.
- **GBIF bounding-box:** broad occurrence candidate pool; needs exact Hays County polygon/locality review.
- **iNaturalist checklist:** local community checklist candidate layer; useful but not equivalent to a voucher.
- **USDA/BPI:** host and plant-pathogen context, especially for rusts, smuts, mildews, leaf spots, and other host-associated fungi.
- **FUNGuild:** ecological guild/function interpretation. It does not prove local occurrence.
- **Central Texas interactions:** host/interaction context. It does not prove local occurrence unless paired with occurrence records.

## Photo policy

The dashboard uses live iNaturalist photo lookup as a public display aid. Photos are not downloaded into the repository. Photo credits and links are shown when the iNaturalist API returns them. A photo should not be treated as proof of nativity or as a substitute for source records.

## Edibility warning

This dashboard is for biodiversity, restoration, and education. It is not an edible mushroom guide. Do not eat wild fungi based on this page.
